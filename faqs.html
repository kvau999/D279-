Frequently Asked Questions

Our “Pexperts” typically encounter the following questions when consulting with prospective “pet parents”:

Questions and Answers

Question: How long can I expect my dog to live based on their breed and size, and what can I do to help them live a healthier life?
Answer: The lifespan of a dog can vary greatly depending on their breed and size. For example, smaller breeds like Chihuahuas can live up to 15-20 years, whereas larger breeds like Great Danes typically live 7-10 years. To help your dog live a healthier life, provide them with a balanced diet, regular exercise, routine veterinary checkups, and plenty of love and mental stimulation.

Question: What’s the best way to treat fleas in my dog’s fur, and how can I prevent them from coming back?
Answer: To treat fleas, use a veterinarian-approved flea shampoo or topical treatment and follow the instructions carefully. Prevent fleas from returning by keeping your pet’s bedding clean, using flea preventatives regularly, and maintaining a clean home environment. Consult your vet for the best options for your dog’s breed and size.

Question: How often should I groom my cat to prevent hairballs, and are there any specific tools or products you recommend?
Answer: Groom your cat at least once a week, or more frequently for long-haired breeds, to reduce shedding and prevent hairballs. Use a slicker brush or grooming gloves to remove loose fur gently. Specialized hairball control cat foods and treats can also help minimize hairball formation.

Question: What are the signs that my senior cat might be experiencing age-related health issues, and when should I consult a vet?
Answer: Signs of age-related health issues in senior cats include weight loss, decreased appetite, increased thirst, changes in litter box habits, and reduced activity levels. If you notice any of these symptoms, consult a veterinarian promptly to address potential health concerns.
